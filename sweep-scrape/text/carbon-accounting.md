FAQs: Carbon accounting at Sweep
What does carbon accounting involve?
Carbon accounting is the process of measuring and reporting an organization’s greenhouse gas emissions. Carbon accounting work involves quantifying greenhouse gas emissions to support sustainability initiatives and regulatory compliance. It includes direct emissions from burning fossil fuels, indirect emissions from purchased electricity, and supply chain emissions known as Scope 3.
Using standard emission factors, companies calculate total GHG emissions in carbon dioxide equivalent (CO₂e) to understand their carbon footprint. Greenhouse gas accounting is a systematic process for measuring and reporting all greenhouse gas emissions, ensuring transparency and supporting climate impact assessments.
Sweep automates the entire carbon accounting process, ensuring consistent alignment with the GHG Protocol and providing accurate, traceable results.
What are the business benefits of carbon accounting?
Effective carbon accounting helps organizations:
Identify where they can reduce emissions and lower energy costs
Strengthen risk management and prepare for climate-related risks
Improve data accuracy and decision-making through real-time emissions data
Build transparency with stakeholders through reliable carbon reporting
Contribute to sustainable development while progressing toward net zero emissions
Enable financial institutions to meet investor expectations and regulatory requirements for ESG reporting and sustainability risk management
With Sweep, carbon accounting enables businesses to move from reporting to real, measurable impact.
Why is a carbon accounting platform useful?
Manual carbon accounting often relies on spreadsheets and inconsistent inputs. A carbon accounting platform like Sweep automates data collection, standardizes emission factors, and delivers accurate carbon accounting at scale.
These platforms can also manage renewable energy certificates (RECs), allowing organizations to attribute cleaner energy sources to their electricity consumption under the market-based method.
It provides a single source of truth for GHG accounting, helping companies manage emissions reporting, maintain data quality, and stay compliant with global sustainability standards. This makes it easier for organizations to measure their carbon dioxide emissions, compare performance over time, and focus on reducing carbon emissions.
What is the difference between carbon accounting and ESG data management?
Carbon accounting focuses exclusively on quantifying and managing greenhouse gas emissions – including carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases such as methane and nitrous oxide. Carbon accounting enables organizations to quantify their carbon footprints for products, operations, and supply chains.
ESG data management encompasses a wider set of sustainability indicators, covering governance, diversity, and social impact. ESG data management involves collecting and leveraging sustainability data for comprehensive reporting and performance improvement.
In practice, accurate corporate carbon accounting provides the foundation for reliable ESG reporting, supplying the carbon data needed for financial accounting, emissions tracking, and net zero planning.
