Securing ESG data – Safeguarding your competitive edge
Category
Blog
Last updated
May 20, 2025
ESG management
In an increasingly interconnected world, the security of digital infrastructure is paramount. The global IT outage on July 19, 2023, triggered by a faulty software update from CrowdStrike, underscored our vulnerability to technological failures. This event, which disrupted airlines, media outlets, banks, and retailers worldwide, serves as a stark reminder of the extensive web of connections that sustain our digital infrastructure.
ESG data has become crucial for enterprises, used to assess sustainability performance, identify risks and opportunities, and address climate change. However, this data often includes sensitive personal information, making its security and privacy paramount. Ensuring the protection of ESG data is not just about compliance but also about maintaining a competitive advantage in a world increasingly focused on sustainability.
Why is environmental, social and governance data valuable?
ESG data encompasses information related to a company’s environmental impact, such as carbon emissions, energy usage, and waste management, as well as social aspects like employee diversity, labor practices, and community engagement. Governance data includes details on corporate leadership, board diversity, executive compensation, and adherence to ethical standards and regulatory compliance.
Robust ESG metrics help businesses identify areas for improvement, manage risks, and enhance their reputation. Leveraging this data effectively can lead to better decision-making, an improved brand reputation, and increased investor confidence. As sustainability becomes an important differentiator, having a strong grasp of ESG data can offer significant competitive advantages. Additionally, aligning with the EU Taxonomy is crucial for regulatory compliance and ensuring that companies’ activities and investments meet the required standards.
What are ESG scores?
ESG scores assess a company’s performance based on environmental, social, and corporate governance criteria. These scores give an overview of how well a company manages its impact on the environment and society, including labor practices and community engagement, and the strength of its governance, including board diversity and corporate ethics. ESG scores provide investors and stakeholders with a measure of how responsibly a company operates, reflecting its commitment to sustainability and ethical practices.
Why is securing ESG data increasingly crucial?
With the growing reliance on ESG data, securing it has become more critical than ever. ESG information often contains sensitive information about employees, customers, and supply chains, making it a prime target for cyber threats. A data breach can lead to severe financial, legal, and reputational damage. Furthermore, compliance with regulations like GDPR and CCPA is mandatory, and failure to protect ESG data can result in substantial fines. As stakeholders demand greater transparency, safeguarding ESG data with full transparency is essential for maintaining trust and meeting regulatory
reporting
requirements.
Why is robust data governance essential?
Robust data governance ensures the integrity, accuracy, and security of ESG data. A solid data governance framework includes clear policies and procedures for managing data, which helps maintain consistency and compliance. It involves setting up data security protocols, privacy standards, and retention guidelines to prevent unauthorized access and breaches. Effective data governance also supports data quality, which is vital for accurate
ESG reporting
and decision-making.
What role can employee training play?
Employee training is crucial for effective ESG data security. Employees are often the first line of defense against data breaches. Comprehensive training programs should cover data security awareness, best practices for data handling, and incident response procedures. Educating employees about potential security risks and proper data management can significantly reduce the likelihood of breaches caused by human error. A well-informed workforce helps ensure that ESG data remains protected and that any security issues are addressed swiftly and effectively.
What are the key certifications to consider?
Certifications are vital indicators of a company’s commitment to data security. Key certifications to look for include:
SOC 2
: Focuses on five trust service criteria—security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy. It ensures that service providers manage data securely.
ISO 27001
: An international standard for information security management systems (ISMS). It provides a systematic approach to managing sensitive information and ensuring its security.
Yannick, Sweep’s CTO, highlights the significance of these certifications: “At Sweep, our SOC 2 compliance and ISO 27001 certification reflect our commitment to maintaining high standards of data security. These certifications assure our clients that their ESG data is secure.”
How to select an ESG data provider with high security standards
Choosing an
ESG data provider
with robust security measures is essential for peace of mind. When evaluating potential providers, consider the following:
Assess certifications
: Verify that the provider holds relevant security certifications, such as SOC 2 and ISO 27001.
Evaluate security policies
: Review the provider’s data security policies and methodology to ensure they align with your company’s standards and regulatory requirements.
Conduct risk assessments
: Understand the provider’s approach to identifying and managing security risks.
Check references and reviews
: Look for feedback from other clients regarding the provider’s security practices and reliability.
Ask the right questions
: During the RFP process, inquire about the provider’s data governance framework, employee training programs, incident response procedures, and technologies used for data protection.
By carefully selecting an ESG data provider
with robust security measures, companies can ensure the protection of their valuable data, thereby maintaining their competitive edge and meeting stakeholder expectations.
