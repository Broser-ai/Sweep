Background
The main company within Groupe Rosa is Linvosges. It was founded in 1923 by Charles Prudent in the Vosges town of Gérardmer, in Eastern France.
For over 100 years, the company’s mission has been to keep French textile tradition and know-how alive, notably via the “Jours Venise” range which is still produced in its Gérardmer studio.
Groupe Rosa has around 350 employees, of which around 300 work on the Linvosges brand. Linvosges textiles are for all parts of the home, with a focus on bed- and bath-linen.
Challenge
In response to growing customer calls for more transparency around Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR), the Group, under the leadership of its President and Linvosges’ CEO, saw a chance to strengthen its already environmentally-conscious business ethos.
As a result, in 2022, Groupe Rosa began tracking its carbon footprint, and created an action plan which was validated by the SBTi. This aligned the Group’s aims with the Paris Agreement and established science-based targets for emissions reduction.
However, Groupe Rosa had never calculated its own carbon footprint before, having previously reported on ESG metrics in spreadsheet format via its investor’s systems.
The company uses various IT tools across its different departments, which meant that the sustainability data logged within them had to be manually collated in order to achieve effective company-level reporting. This left the process open to errors, and placed great time demands on the team responsible for collating the data.
“Sweep streamlines the collation and visualisation of our data. It allows us to centralise everything in one place within our business, instead of on disparate platforms across each department. The platform makes ESG reporting much simpler, both for investors and for our company leadership”
Clémence Guérin
ESG Manager
Solution
Groupe Rosa selected Sweep to achieve greater visibility and control over its carbon footprint tracking and ESG reporting.
The software’s dashboards allow the teams to input their company-level targets, trajectories, and hypotheses, bringing a synergy between departments which was previously lacking.
This data-first approach has proven particularly valuable for the C-Suite and management team, who can now leverage clear visualisations to better understand complex data and make business decisions relating to sustainability objectives.
The improved information flow has reached every level of operations. From executive leadership to frontline teams, the enhanced data transparency and accessibility has empowered all employees to contribute more effectively to company-wide sustainability goals.
The Sweep “Tree” organisation provides an at-a-glance visual representation of all emissions.
Having a central data hub means the Group can collate data from the array of IT tools used across the business, importing it into the Sweep platform with the least human effort. This in turn helps avoid errors and save time.
Groupe Rosa is working to fully automate its data integration into Sweep as of 2025.
