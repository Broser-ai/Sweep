What is the EcoVadis assessment?
The
EcoVadis assessment
process evaluates a company’s sustainability performance against international standards, which is crucial for meeting regulatory requirements and enhancing corporate transparency. The platform transforms complex sustainability data into actionable scorecards that are recognized internationally.
EcoVadis assessments are aligned with global standards like GRI, ISO 26000, and UN Global Compact, aiding companies in meeting legal requirements. This means your EcoVadis scorecard isn’t just a proprietary rating. It’s audit-ready data that aligns with international standards, simplifying compliance efforts with tightening global regulations.
The EcoVadis assessment process involves a detailed questionnaire that evaluates a company’s sustainability performance across four main themes: Environment, Labor & Human Rights, Ethics, and Sustainable Procurement. The EcoVadis scoring system evaluates sustainability performance on a scale of 0 to 100, with scores based on 21 sustainability criteria across these four key themes, which can feed into upcoming
sector-specific CSRD sustainability reporting standards
.
Benefit 1: Win more contracts and strengthen supplier relationships
A strong EcoVadis scorecard helps companies stand out to potential buyers and win contracts, as many large corporations prioritize suppliers with these ratings. If you’re selling to enterprise customers or major retailers, an EcoVadis rating is increasingly a key factor in procurement decisions.
EcoVadis supports buyers in assessing supplier sustainability to reduce risks and enhance supply chain resilience. That means your customers are using EcoVadis to evaluate you whether you participate or not. Without a scorecard, you’re invisible. With a strong score, you’re preferred.
This isn’t theoretical. Companies with strong sustainability credentials are closing deals faster, retaining customers longer, and differentiating themselves in competitive bids. Sustainable procurement practices are now table stakes for doing business with Fortune 500 companies, federal contractors, and global supply chains.
A high EcoVadis rating acts as a trust signal, allowing companies to prove their sustainability claims and reduce accusations of “greenwashing”. In an environment where stakeholders are skeptical of sustainability marketing, third-party verification matters.
Benefit 2: Reduce supply chain risk and build resilience
EcoVadis provides a 360-degree monitoring system to track environmental, social, and ethical risks, which helps companies identify vulnerabilities and avoid disruptions. This applies whether you’re assessing your own suppliers or being assessed by your customers.
Resilient supply chains start with visibility. The EcoVadis assessment process is designed to help companies identify gaps in their sustainability practices and provides customized improvement recommendations based on the submitted documentation.
By evaluating suppliers across the four categories (Environment, Labor and Human Rights, Ethics, and Sustainable Procurement), you can spot risk before it becomes a crisis. That includes labor and human rights violations, modern slavery risks, poor working conditions, environmental impact, and governance practices that expose you to regulatory or reputational harm.
Companies are increasingly required to demonstrate compliance with regulatory requirements through transparent reporting and measurable sustainability practices, which are often assessed through platforms like EcoVadis. If your supplier gets flagged for violations, that’s your risk too. EcoVadis helps you manage it proactively.
Benefit 3: Meet evolving regulatory requirements
The regulatory landscape for compliance is evolving rapidly, with new regulations such as the
Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD)
and the Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation (SFDR) being implemented across Europe. While these are European regulations, they affect US companies doing business in the EU or working with European suppliers and customers, especially as
CSRD requirements for US companies
and the broader
impact of the EU’s CSRD on US businesses
come into force.
California’s climate disclosure laws (
SB 253
and SB 261) are raising the bar domestically. Federal contractors face increasing sustainability criteria. Investors are demanding ESG data. The compliance burden is growing, and EcoVadis offers a structured approach to meet it.
EcoVadis assessments provide audit-ready data that aligns with international standards, simplifying compliance efforts with tightening global regulations. Instead of building separate reporting processes for every framework, you complete one EcoVadis assessment and use that trusted data across multiple regulatory requirements, including preparing for comprehensive
CSRD-aligned sustainability disclosures
.
The EcoVadis evaluation process provides actionable insights for improvement over time, including strengths and areas for development, which can complement evolving requirements under frameworks like the
Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive for UK companies
. That means your sustainability journey is documented, measurable, and auditable.
Benefit 4: Improve sustainability performance with actionable insights
The EcoVadis assessment isn’t a pass/fail test. It’s a diagnostic tool. After submission, EcoVadis experts analyze the provided information and documentation, which usually takes about 6 to 8 weeks, to deliver a detailed scorecard highlighting strengths and areas for improvement.
To improve your EcoVadis score, it is essential to review your scorecard and identify areas where points were not awarded, focusing on the largest gaps in performance. EcoVadis offers specific, actionable insights tied to the four key themes and 21 sustainability criteria.
This feedback helps you prioritize. Instead of trying to fix everything at once, you can focus on improvement areas that will have the biggest impact on your overall score and your actual sustainability performance.
A strategic approach to sustainability, including setting SMART goals based on EcoVadis feedback, can significantly enhance a company’s performance in future assessments. Continuous improvement is built into the process. You’re not just checking a box. You’re building better practices.
Benefit 5: Build trust with investors and customers
Companies with strong sustainability ratings are often viewed more favorably by investors, as these ratings are linked to lower risks and better long-term financial returns. A high EcoVadis rating validates sustainability commitments to stakeholders, investors, and customers.
The demand for sustainability ratings is growing as businesses face increasing pressure from regulators and stakeholders to demonstrate their commitment to sustainable practices and responsible governance, particularly under financial disclosure rules like the
Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation (SFDR)
and evolving
European Commission guidance on SFDR implementation
. Investors want proof. Customers want transparency. Employees want to work for ethical businesses.
A high EcoVadis scorecard provides that proof. It shows your company’s commitment to corporate social responsibility, not through marketing language, but through verified performance data.
What the EcoVadis assessment process looks like
Understanding the EcoVadis assessment process helps you prepare effectively and maximize your score.
Step 1: Complete the EcoVadis questionnaire
The EcoVadis assessment is based on international standards like GRI and ISO 26000, using a customized questionnaire and document verification tailored to a company’s industry, size, and location. The detailed questionnaire evaluates your company’s sustainability practices across environmental impact, labor and human rights, ethics and sustainable procurement, and governance practices.
Companies typically need to budget 6 to 10 weeks to prepare their EcoVadis submission, which includes gathering documentation and completing the questionnaire. This isn’t something you complete in an afternoon. It requires coordination across teams and functions.
Step 2: Submit supporting documents
Companies should ensure that their documentation meets EcoVadis’ stringent requirements, as frequently rejected documents can hinder the assessment process. Your submitted documentation must be current, relevant, and demonstrate actual practices, not just policies.
Examples of supporting documents include sustainability reports, certifications (ISO 14001, SA8000, etc.), policies on waste management, carbon emissions, supplier engagement, and evidence of compliance with various aspects of sustainability criteria.
To ensure accuracy, assign clear ownership of each section of the EcoVadis response and involve subject matter experts from environmental, HR, procurement, and legal teams.
Step 3: Receive your EcoVadis scorecard
Your final score is based on how well your submitted documentation and EcoVadis questionnaire responses demonstrate performance across the four categories. The overall score places you in a percentile ranking compared to industry peers.
The scorecard shows your company’s performance in each theme, improvement areas, and how you compare to other companies in your sector.
How to improve your EcoVadis score
Getting certified is step one.
Improving your score
over time is where the real value lies.
1. Review your scorecard and identify gaps
To improve your EcoVadis score, it is essential to review your scorecard and identify areas where points were not awarded, focusing on the largest gaps in performance. Don’t just look at your overall score. Drill into each theme and understand where you lost points.
2. Set SMART goals based on feedback
A strategic approach to sustainability, including setting SMART goals based on EcoVadis feedback, can significantly enhance a company’s performance in future assessments. Use the actionable insights from your scorecard to prioritize initiatives with the biggest impact.
3. Improve documentation quality
Many companies lose points not because they lack sustainability practices, but because their documentation doesn’t meet EcoVadis’ requirements. Ensure your supporting documents are specific, recent, and demonstrate implementation, not just intent.
4. Track progress and reassess
The EcoVadis evaluation process provides actionable insights for improvement over time, including strengths and areas for development. Sustainability performance improves through continuous improvement, not one-time fixes. Track your initiatives, measure results, and reassess regularly.
Is EcoVadis right for your business?
EcoVadis is particularly valuable if:
You sell to large corporations or global supply chains that require sustainability ratings
You’re facing increasing regulatory requirements and need audit-ready sustainability data
You want to identify and manage risks across your own supply chain
You need a recognized, third-party validation of your sustainability efforts
You’re competing for contracts where sustainability credentials are a key factor
EcoVadis assesses supply chain sustainability by evaluating companies across four key themes: Environment, Labor & Human Rights, Ethics, and Sustainable Procurement, which helps organizations understand their performance and manage risks effectively.
If any of these apply, an EcoVadis assessment is worth the investment.
