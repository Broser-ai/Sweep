Sweep CEO Rachel Delacour receives Berkeley World Business Analytics Awards
Last updated
September 24, 2024
News announcement
The award recognizes purpose-driven projects led by women with an expertise in applied business analytics.
It’s her work building carbon management platform Sweep that led
Rachel Delacour
to receive the
Berkeley World Business Analytics Awards
in the Project of the Year 2022 category.
Led by
Berkeley’s Fisher Center for Business Analytics
, this award celebrates relevant, ethical, and humane analytics work by recognizing women in applied business analytics, projects that impact social and environmental issues, and innovative work.
After the acquisition of her first startup, Rachel Delacour put her business analytics skills in service of climate change. Together with cofounders Raphael Güller, Yannick Chaze, and Nicolas Raspal, they joined forces with leading climate experts Renaud Bettin and Marie-Anne Vincent to build Sweep, Europe’s top carbon management platform that helps organizations get their emissions on-track.
The Berkeley’s Fisher Center advances the creation of business value from data science through research, education, and accelerating the diffusion and adoption of analytics throughout all aspects of business and management.
Read about Sweep’s unique
data approach to carbon management
.
Sweep can help
Sweep makes sustainability work for your business. Not the other way round. We connect all your sustainability data and turn it into business intelligence to help you unlock performance – from compliance and risk reduction, all the way to cost-savings, and market differentiation.
With Sweep, you can:
Lower costs through real-time tracking and insights
Strengthen supply chains with end-to-end visibility and engagement
Deliver audit-ready sustainability and climate reporting with confidence
Make sustainability intelligence available to everyone to optimize the business
