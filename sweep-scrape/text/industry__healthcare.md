Data management is a unique challenge for the healthcare sector
Driving sustainability in healthcare comes with several key challenges, starting with the need for a responsible and secure supply chain. But by engaging all key stakeholders, from suppliers to researchers, healthcare companies can meet their ESG targets and support partners in achieving theirs. Sweep empowers healthcare providers to implement a collaborative reduction strategy.
Book a demo
Supply chain ethics
Ensure an ethical and sustainable supply chain, particularly during health crises, is vital. Sourcing, fair labor practices, and supply chain integrity are addressed.
Environmental impact
Pharmaceutical manufacturing often has a significant environmental footprint. Reduce waste, energy use, and emissions while disposing of drugs and medical waste responsibly.
Responsible innovation
Conducting rigorous, ethical research and addressing a new drug’s environmental impacts is critical. Balance innovation with responsibility.
Healthcare’s climate footprint is 4.4% of the global total;
meaning if it were a country, it would be the fifth largest emitter on the planet.
Source:
Arup Report on Healthcare’s Climate Footprint
