In this guide, you will learn:
How to start carbon accounting
, even with limited data
When to use spend-based vs. activity-based calculations
How to improve data accuracy
for better reporting

with key regulations
How to turn carbon data into an effective transition plan
Carbon accounting can feel overwhelming for service firms – whether you run a SaaS platform, consultancy, law firm, or financial services business. Many worry their data isn’t complete or precise enough. The truth is:
no one starts with perfect data
. What matters is
raising your data maturity over time
.
According to Morgan Stanley
, 88% of companies globally now view sustainability as a long-term value driver. For service firms, where much of your footprint comes from your supply chains, travel, and digital infrastructure, getting control of that data can unlock real financial upside.
This guide is built especially for service organizations. It will help you:
Improve data accuracy and consistency, especially across suppliers and IT infrastructure
Shift from spend-based proxies to activity-based calculations
Stay ahead of evolving regulations
Turn insights into meaningful action
And with real-world examples from service and mixed-sector companies that have faced these challenges, you’ll gain clarity and confidence to map a path forward.
