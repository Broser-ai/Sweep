FAQs: Workiva vs Sweep
Is Workiva or Sweep better for measuring carbon footprint and greenhouse gas emissions?
In the Workiva vs Sweep comparison, both platforms help companies measure their carbon footprint and greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions across Scope 1, 2, and 3.
Sweep is a full-lifecycle carbon accounting platform built for comprehensive emissions tracking, automated data collection, and sustainability data management. It aligns with the best carbon accounting methods under the GHG Protocol, enabling precise emissions measurement, data accuracy, and transparent carbon data across complex supply chains.
Workiva integrates carbon accounting into its ESG reporting software, combining environmental, social, and governance (ESG) data with financial reporting, audit readiness, and data transparency. It provides strong data management, collaboration tools, and reporting capabilities, but may need extensions for advanced carbon accounting software functionality.
If you need a carbon management platform focused on emissions reporting, data collection, and climate management, Sweep offers more advanced carbon accounting software platforms; Workiva is ideal for ESG reporting and integrated financial controls.
Which platform supports audit-ready data and regulatory compliance?
Sweep is designed to help organisations meet UK climate regulations including SECR, UK SRS, and CSRD, providing audit ready data, streamlined data collection, and reliable data for sustainability reporting. It produces audit ready reports, ensures data quality, and supports multiple frameworks and reporting frameworks. Its focus on data transparency, risk management, and audit readiness makes it one of the best carbon accounting software solutions for regulatory requirements.
Workiva also offers strong ESG software for audit-ready data and reporting frameworks, especially suited to financial institutions and enterprises under major ESG frameworks. It connects ESG metrics, environmental data, and financial performance for consistent sustainability reporting and performance management.
In short, Sweep emphasises automated data collection and supplier engagement for carbon emissions compliance, while Workiva excels at governance, audit controls, and integrated financial and ESG reporting.
How do Workiva and Sweep compare for data collection, value chain, and complex supply-chain emissions?
Sweep delivers automated data collection and customizable surveys to capture indirect emissions and supply chain compliance data from across the value chain. It eliminates manual data capture, fills data gaps, and ensures data quality and data accuracy through structured workflows. Its carbon accounting software enables comprehensive emissions tracking, emissions data analysis, and environmental impact assessments across global operations.
Workiva provides data collection and reporting tools that connect ESG data from multiple departments and external systems, improving data transparency and reporting processes. It supports collaboration tools, customizable dashboards, and audit ready reports, but its supply chain emissions tracking relies more on integration with external carbon accounting tools.
If your goal is streamlined data collection, carbon management, and visibility across complex supply chains, Sweep provides more advanced data analytics and sustainability performance tracking; Workiva is best for connecting ESG reporting and financial reporting workflows.
Which platform is better for scenario modelling and reduction targets toward net zero?
Sweep offers built-in scenario analysis, reduction targets, and climate management tools that transform carbon data into actionable insights for decarbonization strategies. It supports science-based targets, models future carbon emissions, and helps organisations reduce emissions and track net zero emissions progress. Its real time data analysis and sustainability tools help companies make informed decisions and identify cost savings opportunities while managing climate-related risks.
Workiva supports climate risk management and basic climate risk analytics within its ESG reporting software, linking environmental data to financial performance and risk management. It’s strong for embedding net zero goals within enterprise reporting and ensuring consistency across report ESG data and governance.
Sweep is ideal for organisations wanting to connect climate data with real-world emissions reductions and net zero progress; Workiva suits those needing enterprise-level reporting frameworks with governance and oversight.
What type of organisation should choose Sweep over Workiva (or vice versa)?
Sweep is best for large UK and global enterprises, including financial institutions, with complex supply chains, multiple business units, and ambitious sustainability goals. It provides one of the best carbon accounting software platforms for data collection, emissions tracking, and regulatory compliance under SECR, UK SRS, and CSRD. It helps companies track progress, reduce emissions, and improve ESG performance through data transparency and environmental intelligence.
Workiva is better suited for organisations focused on integrated ESG and financial reporting, risk management, and audit ready reports. It excels in connecting ESG data, financial reporting, and major ESG frameworks in a single system, improving reporting capabilities and collaboration with external stakeholders and investors.
Many enterprises combine both: Sweep for operational carbon management and sustainability data accuracy, and Workiva for enterprise-level governance, audit readiness, and ESG reporting to external partners.
