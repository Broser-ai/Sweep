ESG management
If you’re leading sustainability efforts at a FTSE 250 company, you’re navigating increasingly complex terrain. The UK Sustainability Reporting Standard (UK SRS) came into effect in February 2026, and many UK businesses face dual reporting requirements if they operate EU subsidiaries subject to the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD).
According to
Sweep’s 2026 State of Sustainability in UK Businesses report
,
72% of UK companies are not fully prepared
for these new reporting frameworks. Meanwhile,
78% still rely on spreadsheets
for carbon accounting, and
83% struggle with supplier data collection
, the single biggest challenge in sustainability data management.
The message is clear: manual processes and disconnected tools cannot scale to meet today’s regulatory requirements and stakeholder expectations.
Why sustainability software is essential for corporate sustainability goals
ESG and sustainability reporting software assists organisations in collecting, managing, and disclosing data on ESG criteria, which is crucial for reporting sustainability performance and ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements.
The business case extends beyond compliance.
Sweep’s UK survey
reveals that companies with robust sustainability data systems see tangible results:
67% report better decision-making
through improved carbon data quality
52% achieve cost savings
from energy efficiency and optimised operations
58% gain enhanced brand reputation
43% build improved supplier relationships
Effective software for managing business sustainability in 2026 centres on automating data collection, ensuring regulatory compliance such as CSRD, and tracking carbon emissions across all scopes.
What to look for when selecting sustainability management software
Selecting the best ESG reporting software for an organisation involves defining specific reporting needs, researching potential vendors, evaluating features and functionality, and assessing data accuracy and validation capabilities.
Here are the critical capabilities your sustainability management platform should provide:
1. Comprehensive data collection across the entire value chain
Companies that actively engage their suppliers in sustainability practices can achieve better compliance with environmental regulations and improve their overall ESG performance.
Your software tools must handle emissions data from Scope 1, 2, and 3 sources.
Effective carbon management involves tracking Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions across the entire value chain
, which is critical for organisations aiming to reduce their overall carbon footprint.
Look for platforms that:
Automate data collection from internal and external stakeholders
Integrate with existing systems (ERP, facilities management, Microsoft Sustainability Cloud)
Provide supplier portals for streamlined supply chain engagement
Support diverse data formats to structure data effectively
2. Built-in regulatory compliance frameworks
Built-in frameworks ensure reports comply with legal requirements like the CSRD, saving time on interpreting complex regulations.
Your sustainability reporting software should support:
UK SRS
requirements
CSRD
for EU operations
Global Reporting Initiative (GRI)
Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation (SFDR)
International Sustainability Standards Board (ISSB) guidelines
Compliance with sustainability reporting standards is essential for organisations to meet regulatory requirements and stakeholder expectations.
The right platform keeps pace with regulatory change so you don’t have to rebuild processes with each update.
3. Accurate carbon accounting and carbon footprint calculations
Carbon management software is essential for organisations to measure, manage, and report their carbon emissions, helping them quantify greenhouse gas emissions and support strategic decision-making for carbon reduction initiatives.
Your platform should provide:
Verified emission factors from recognised databases
Automated carbon footprint calculations
Transparent methodology and audit trails
Data accuracy validation to ensure reliable reporting
4. Actionable intelligence for reduction planning
Decarbonisation software is essential for businesses to plan and implement strategies aimed at reducing carbon emissions and transitioning to low-carbon operations.
Look beyond basic reporting capabilities to platforms that:
Identify carbon hotspots across operations
Model reduction scenarios and target setting
Track progress against sustainability goals
Provide insights to pinpoint improvement areas
Effective climate transition planning involves identifying reduction levers and utilising software tools to analyse emissions data across the value chain
, which is critical for achieving sustainability goals.
5. Stakeholder confidence through transparency
Transparent, verified reports build confidence among investors, customers, and regulators.
Your management platform should:
Maintain comprehensive audit trails
Store documents and data within one platform
Support third-party verification processes
Enable consistent reporting processes across reporting periods
6. Scalability and ease of use
With
44% of UK businesses lacking sufficient internal resources
for sustainability reporting, your software must:
Reduce manual effort through automation
Provide intuitive interfaces that don’t require extensive training
Scale as your sustainability initiatives grow
Support cross-functional teams across procurement, operations, and facilities

Who it’s for:

2. Watershed
Best for:
Technology-forward companies prioritising carbon footprint reduction with strong science-based target integration
Key strengths:
Clean, intuitive interface that simplifies complex carbon management
Strong focus on reduction planning and scenario modelling
Excellent educational resources and climate expertise
Good integration capabilities with common data sources
Considerations:
Primarily US-focused, with evolving UK/EU regulatory coverage
May require supplementary tools for comprehensive CSRD compliance
Pricing can be significant for mid-market organisations
Who it’s for:
Companies with strong internal sustainability teams seeking sophisticated carbon management tools and science-based reduction strategies.
3. Greenly
Best for:
Small to mid-sized businesses seeking accessible entry points to carbon accounting
Key strengths:
User-friendly platform suitable for teams new to sustainability reporting
Competitive pricing for smaller organisations
Quick implementation and onboarding
Good for basic Scope 1, 2, and limited Scope 3 tracking
Considerations:
Limited functionality for complex, multi-site operations typical of FTSE 250
Supplier engagement capabilities less developed
May outgrow platform as sustainability maturity increases
Who it’s for:
Growing businesses establishing their first formal carbon accounting processes, or FTSE 250 subsidiaries needing simpler tools for specific divisions.
4. Persefoni
Best for:
Financial services and investment firms requiring portfolio-level carbon accounting
Key strengths:
Sophisticated financial-grade data management
Strong portfolio carbon footprint tracking for asset managers
Comprehensive audit trail and data governance
Alignment with financial reporting standards
Considerations:
Complexity can create steeper learning curve
Implementation typically requires significant time and resources
Pricing positioned at enterprise premium level
Who it’s for:
Financial institutions, asset managers, and large corporations where finance teams lead ESG reporting and integration with financial systems is paramount.
5. Sphera
Best for:
Heavy industry and manufacturing with established EHS (Environment, Health, Safety) programmes
Key strengths:
Deep environmental management and environmental footprint tracking
Strong operational risk management integration
Extensive experience in regulated industries
Comprehensive product lifecycle capabilities
Considerations:
Platform can feel complex for organisations focused purely on carbon and ESG
Implementation and configuration typically resource-intensive
May include functionality beyond sustainability teams’ core needs
Who it’s for:
Large industrial companies, particularly in manufacturing, chemicals, and energy sectors, seeking to integrate sustainability with broader EHS and risk management programmes.
6. Salesforce Net Zero Cloud
Best for:
Existing Salesforce customers seeking to add sustainability to their CRM ecosystem
Key strengths:
Native integration with Salesforce CRM and data ecosystem
Familiar interface for Salesforce users
Leverages existing Salesforce infrastructure investment
Good for linking sustainability to customer and stakeholder engagement
Considerations:
Requires Salesforce ecosystem, less suitable as standalone solution
Carbon accounting depth may be lighter than specialist platforms
Customisation often needed to match specific regulatory frameworks
Who it’s for:
Organisations heavily invested in Salesforce infrastructure looking to extend their platform rather than adopt separate sustainability solutions.
Making the right choice for your organisation
Utilising carbon management software can enhance operational efficiency, improve compliance with regulations, and bolster a company’s competitive edge by providing insights into emissions data and reduction strategies.
The right sustainability management software should:
Automate what’s manual today
– Moving beyond spreadsheets isn’t optional. Look for platforms that reduce the manual effort dragging down your team.
Solve your supplier challenge
–
Engaging suppliers in sustainability initiatives can lead to improved performance and reduced environmental impact across the entire value chain.
Choose tools with robust supplier engagement strategies, including portals, standardised templates, and automated workflows.
Support dual reporting
– If you face both UK SRS and CSRD requirements, ensure your platform supports both frameworks without duplicating effort.
Drive business value
– The 67% seeing better decision-making and 52% achieving cost savings prove that sustainability intelligence should inform strategic decisions across operations, procurement, and facilities management.
Grow with you
– Your sustainability journey will evolve. Select comprehensive solutions that scale from compliance to strategic sustainability performance optimisation.

With UK SRS reporting beginning for fiscal years starting April 2026, and
72% of businesses not fully prepared
, delaying platform selection creates unnecessary risk. The organisations that will thrive are those treating sustainability data management as strategic infrastructure, not a compliance checkbox.
Companies that actively engage their suppliers in sustainability practices can achieve better compliance with environmental regulations and improve their overall ESG performance.
The right platform makes this engagement systematic rather than sporadic.
Compliance is the floor, not the ceiling.
The real opportunity lies in using sustainability data to mitigate risks, track progress, improve transparency, and unlock value across your operations. From pinpoint improvement areas to informed decisions about reduction planning, your sustainability platform should play a vital role in your broader business strategy.
Modern sustainability reporting software delivers reporting capabilities that transform raw data into meaningful insights. It enables you to deliver audit-ready sustainability and climate reporting with confidence, strengthen stakeholder trust, and make sustainability intelligence available across your organisation.
The question isn’t whether to invest in dedicated sustainability management software. It’s which platform will help you not just comply, but compete and lead in the low-carbon economy.
