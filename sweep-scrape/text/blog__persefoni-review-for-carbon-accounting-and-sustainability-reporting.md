Persefoni Review for Carbon Accounting and Sustainability Reporting

Persefoni is a carbon accounting and sustainability reporting platform designed to help organizations measure emissions, manage climate disclosures, and build more structured reporting processes across Scope 1, 2, and 3 categories.
The platform is used by organizations that manage regulatory climate disclosures, investor reporting, financed emissions and enterprise carbon accounting programs. The question is, is it the right fit for your organization?
This review explores how Persefoni works, the capabilities it offers for carbon accounting and sustainability reporting, and where it may or may not fit depending on how sustainability is managed within the organization.
What is Persefoni and who is it best suited for?
Persefoni is a carbon accounting and climate disclosure management platform.
It is typically used in environments where emissions reporting must be consistent, auditable, and aligned with financial and regulatory processes. This includes organizations that manage investor disclosures, climate reporting requirements, financed-emissions analysis, and enterprise-scale carbon accounting programs.
The software supports sustainability and finance teams by:
Calculating emissions across
Scope 1, 2, and detailed Scope 3 categories
using standardized methodologies
Applying emissions factors and models aligned with the GHG Protocol
Structuring carbon data for regulatory disclosures and investor reporting
Maintaining audit trails and controls for emissions data and calculations
Supporting financed emissions measurement and analytics for financial services organizations
With Persefoni, organizations build a more structured and defensible foundation for carbon accounting and sustainability reporting.
Persefoni’s core features and capabilities
Carbon footprint measurement and Scope 3 accounting
Carbon accounting
sits at the center of the Persefoni platform. The software supports emissions measurement across Scope 1 and 2, as well as detailed Scope 3 categories, using standardized methodologies aligned with the GHG Protocol.
The platform is designed to help organizations structure emissions data across complex operational environments, including supplier networks, purchased goods and services, financed emissions, and value chain activities.
Sustainability and regulatory reporting
Persefoni supports sustainability reporting across a range of climate disclosure requirements and investor reporting processes. The platform is designed to help organizations produce more structured and defensible emissions disclosures using centralized carbon data and audit-ready reporting controls.
This includes support for reporting requirements linked to frameworks and regulations such as
CSRD
, SEC climate disclosures, and California climate legislation.
PersefoniAI and emissions intelligence
Persefoni includes embedded AI capabilities through PersefoniAI and Persefoni Copilot. These tools are designed to support emissions analysis, anomaly detection, emissions factor mapping, and carbon accounting workflows.
The AI functionality is positioned around improving efficiency and confidence in emissions reporting processes, particularly where organizations are managing large datasets across multiple emissions categories and operational systems.
Decarbonization planning and supplier engagement
Beyond emissions reporting, Persefoni also supports
decarbonization planning
and supplier engagement workflows. Organizations can use the platform to model reduction initiatives, support net-zero target setting, and improve visibility into supplier emissions data across Scope 3 categories.
This helps sustainability teams move beyond measurement alone and build a clearer view of where emissions reduction opportunities exist across the business and supply chain.
Financial services and financed emissions capabilities
Persefoni also provides dedicated capabilities for financial services organizations
managing financed emissions
and portfolio-level climate reporting requirements.
These tools support financed emissions measurement, portfolio company engagement, investor reporting, and emissions analytics aligned with financial services disclosure requirements and climate reporting frameworks.
Notable Persefoni case study
Natera, a clinical genetic testing company with more than 3,000 employees across 18 locations, partnered with Persefoni to improve the accuracy, transparency, and auditability of its carbon accounting and ESG reporting processes.
Before implementing the platform, Natera’s sustainability team faced challenges in managing emissions data using spreadsheets and manual processes while responding to increasing stakeholder and regulatory expectations for climate disclosures.
Persefoni was implemented to help Natera:
Calculate Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions across multiple sites and operational activities
Improve confidence in ESG reporting and disclosure readiness
Create a more transparent and auditable carbon accounting process
Streamline data collection across internal teams and stakeholders
Support long-term emissions tracking and reduction planning with more reliable historical data
The example highlights Persefoni’s strengths. The software is a good fit for organizations that need a more structured and defensible approach to carbon accounting, climate disclosures, and emissions reporting.
Potential limitations and considerations
Persefoni is designed primarily around
carbon accounting
and climate reporting. Organizations looking for broader sustainability management capabilities across procurement, operations, supply chain collaboration, and ESG workflows outside emissions reporting may still require additional systems or processes alongside the platform.
Because the platform supports detailed Scope 1, 2, and 3 accounting across complex reporting environments, implementation and data management processes can become more involved as emissions categories, reporting requirements, and supplier datasets expand.
Review feedback commonly highlights the platform’s strong support and structured approach to carbon accounting. Some users also note that while the platform simplifies complex emissions management processes, parts of the interface and navigation can take time to become familiar with when managing detailed reporting workflows.
