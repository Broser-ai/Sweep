## Executive Summary
The EU sustainability regulatory landscape is undergoing rapid consolidation: CSRD scope has been significantly narrowed to 1,000+ employee / EUR 450M+ turnover entities, CBAM entered its definitive phase January 2026 with a new 50-tonne small-importer exemption, and CSDDD application is delayed to July 2029 at higher thresholds (5,000 employees / EUR 1.5B turnover). Digital Product Passports are real and imminent, with batteries as the first mandated category by February 2027 under already-adopted regulation, and textiles/iron/steel/aluminium following through 2028-2030. Denmark's green tax reform is already in force (1 January 2025) with rates climbing to DKK 750/tonne (non-ETS) and DKK 375/tonne (ETS) by 2030. On the technology side, Sphera and VelocityEHS offer the most unified factory environmental compliance platforms, Microsoft Sustainability Manager's Copilot AI features remain in preview (not production-ready), Circularise leads DPP infrastructure with blockchain and zero-knowledge proofs, and GHGSat is actively expanding its satellite constellation toward daily-revisit methane monitoring by end of 2026.

## Caveats
1. TIME-SENSITIVITY: EU regulatory timelines are in active flux. The Omnibus I/II packages, Stop-the-Clock Directive, and CBAM simplification were all adopted in 2025-2026 and may be further amended. The CSRD scope narrowing and CSDDD threshold increases are politically contested and could be revisited.

2. SOURCE LIMITATIONS: Several claims rely on vendor websites (Sphera, VelocityEHS, Circularise, GHGSat) as primary sources. While these are appropriate for describing a company's own products, they carry inherent marketing bias. Independent analyst validation (Verdantix) exists for Sphera but not for all vendors.

3. COVERAGE GAPS: The verified claims cover only a subset of the original research question. Major ESG platforms (Sweep, Watershed, Persefoni, Workiva, Greenly, Plan A, Normative, Salesforce Net Zero Cloud, Enablon, SAP) were not represented in the surviving verified claims. The Verdantix Green Quadrant claim that would have provided comparative rankings was refuted (1-2 vote). Similarly, IBM Envizi, Google Carbon Sense, and most AI agent capabilities lack verified claims.

4. ALUMINIUM TIMELINE DISCREPANCY: The PassportCraft source places the aluminium delegated act in 2028, but Sidley Austin and OneclickLCA place it in 2027. This specific date should be independently verified before acting on it.

5. MICROSOFT COPILOT PREVIEW STATUS: As of mid-2026, all Copilot features in Microsoft Sustainability Manager remain in preview. The CSRD report generation capability scaffolds reports from existing data rather than generating substantive compliance content -- the word "generate" in marketing materials overstates what the tool actually produces.

6. DANISH TAX REFORM REVIEW: The DKK 125/tonne mineralogical process rate is subject to planned government reviews in 2026 and 2028 and may change.

7. REFUTED CLAIMS: Six claims were refuted, including important ones about CBAM full payment obligations, CSDDD tier-1-only due diligence scope, and the EU Central DPP Registry going live July 2026. These refutations highlight how fast regulatory details are changing and how secondary sources lag behind amendments.
